Ahmed                     Dini 
        Sagaal nin oo walaalo ah sideennaas  
        Siddeed isku bah tahay sidiinnaas  
        Ninkuna bah gaar yahay sidaydaas  
        Siddeedduna ninkii siri sidiinnaas  
        Ninkuna sirtii garan sidaydaas  
        Haddaynu isa siri siddeeddaba  
        Mayska kala tagnaa sideedaba  
                   
By Sayid Mohamed Abdulle Hassan Buuga Gabayada Sayidka


Nabad — Peace
Common greeting and concept meaning tranquility and safety. Example: “Nabad miyaa?” (“Is there peace?”).
Aqoon — Knowledge
Refers to learned skill or understanding. Often praised in proverbs: “Aqoon la’aani waa iftiin la’aan.”
Rajo — Hope
Optimistic expectation for the future; used in encouragement during hardship.
Wadajir — Unity
Standing together as one. Frequently used in community and nation-building contexts.
Geesinimo — Bravery
Courage in the face of danger or difficulty; admired personal quality in poetry and history.
Caafimaad — Health
Physical and mental well-being. Often paired with “nasiino” (rest) in everyday advice.
Qoys — Family
Immediate family unit; central to Somali social life and responsibilities.
Saaxiib — Friend
A companion or close friend; plural “saaxiibbo.” Used for both casual and deep bonds.
Dhaqan — Culture
Customs, traditions, and shared social values; includes poetry, hospitality, and pastoral life.
Xoog — Strength
Physical or figurative power; can describe muscle, conviction, or intensity.
Himilo — Ambition
Personal or collective aspiration; often linked to education and career goals.
Hodan — Wealth
Material abundance or prosperity; also a common female name meaning “wealthy.”
Nolol — Life
Existence or livelihood; used broadly from daily routines to philosophical talk.
Roob — Rain
Vital seasonal rainfall; central to pastoral cycles and blessings: “Roob waa raxmad.”
Hilaac — Lightning
Flash of electricity during storms; often paired with “onkod” (thunder).
Asluub — Manners
Good conduct and etiquette; taught to children and praised in social settings.
Deeqsinimo — Generosity
Giving freely to others; a highly valued trait tied to hospitality.
Samir — Patience
Calm endurance through difficulty; common in advice: “Samir iyo iman.”
Gobannimo — Freedom / Dignity
Independence and honor; a historical and moral ideal celebrated in poetry.
Dal — Country
Nation or homeland; used in patriotic contexts and geography (“dalkeenna” = our country).
